Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish known for its rich layers of flavor and hearty texture.
It typically consists of wide, flat pasta sheets that form the base and structure of the dish.
These sheets are boiled until al dente, providing the perfect foundation for the various fillings and sauces that make lasagna so delectable.

One of the most traditional versions of lasagna includes a robust meat sauce made from ground beef or sausage.
This sauce is typically prepared by simmering the meat with tomatoes, onions, garlic, and a blend of Italian herbs such as oregano, basil, and thyme.
The result is a savory and aromatic sauce that infuses the dish with deep, comforting flavors.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon white sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • pepper to taste
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Combine ground beef, onion, garlic, and remaining ingredients in a large skillet.
  3. Bake for 15 minutes or until meat is browned and bubbly.
  4. Remove meat from skillet and let stand for 5 minutes.
  5. Combine sauce ingredients in a bowl and mix well.
  6. Pour sauce over lasagna and bake for 30 minutes.
